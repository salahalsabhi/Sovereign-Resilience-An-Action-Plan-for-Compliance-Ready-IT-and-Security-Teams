## Overview

This repository contains my notes and key takeaways from the webinar:

**Sovereign Resilience: An Action Plan for Compliance-Ready IT and Security Teams**

Hosted by **Acronis** on **June 16, 2026**, this session explored how organizations can build security and operational resilience while meeting modern regulatory and compliance requirements.

The webinar focused on practical security controls, audit readiness, incident response preparedness, disaster recovery, and compliance frameworks such as NIS2, DORA, NIST, GDPR, HIPAA, and SOX.

## Key Topics Covered

### Regulatory Compliance

The session highlighted how organizations are moving from compliance based on documentation toward compliance based on demonstrable operational controls.

Frameworks discussed included:

* NIS2
* DORA
* NIST
* GDPR
* HIPAA
* SOX

---

### Cyber Resilience

Organizations must be able to:

* Detect security incidents
* Preserve forensic evidence
* Recover business operations
* Demonstrate accountability
* Maintain audit readiness

Cyber resilience was presented as a core business requirement rather than a purely technical objective.

---

### Evidence Preservation & Forensic Readiness

Important capabilities include:

* Immutable storage
* Audit logging
* Evidence retention
* Incident reconstruction
* Digital forensics support

These controls help organizations meet regulatory expectations during investigations and audits.

---

### Disaster Recovery & Recoverability

The webinar emphasized the importance of:

* Backup integrity
* Recovery testing
* Business continuity planning
* Disaster recovery procedures

Regulators increasingly expect organizations to prove recoverability, not simply claim it.

---

### Security Monitoring & Visibility

Effective compliance requires:

* Continuous monitoring
* Centralized logging
* Endpoint visibility
* Threat detection
* Audit trail generation

Visibility enables both security operations and compliance reporting.

---

## Key Takeaways

* Compliance is no longer just documentation.
* Organizations must demonstrate security controls in practice.
* Evidence preservation is critical for investigations and audits.
* Cyber resilience is a regulatory expectation.
* Incident response and disaster recovery capabilities directly support compliance objectives.
* Security and compliance should be integrated rather than treated as separate functions.

## Conclusion

This webinar provided valuable insight into the relationship between cybersecurity operations and regulatory compliance. Modern security programs must not only prevent attacks but also demonstrate accountability, recoverability, and resilience under audit and investigation scenarios.

Understanding these concepts helps security professionals align technical controls with business, legal, and regulatory requirements.
